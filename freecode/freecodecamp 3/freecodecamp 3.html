Your Girlfriend© is designed to be the most adorable human and has for purpose to make you happy.

Feed Your Girlfriend

Your Girlfriend© seems off ? Might mean she needs a meal !

Remember to feed your Girlfriend© every day, multiple times. Your Girlfriend© has nutritionary needs you should fulfill to keep her in function.

How to know your Girlfriend© is hungry

  • She told you she was hungry
  • She didn't eat today
  • She is sad
  • She is grumpy
  • She starts arguments without reasons
  • She doesn't talk
  • Cuddles do not help
  • She is pacing around
  • She has a knife and starts menacing you

Chocolate, pasta pesto, kebab, pizza, salad, sandwiches... Your Girlfriend© can eat everything you eat, but remember to avoid any seafood.

else:
    mention=input("Did your Girlfriend© asked if you'd like something specific ? (yes/no)").lower()
    if mention=="yes":
        specific=input("What exactly ?").lower()
        print("Your Girlfriend© is hungry, she wants to eat", specific, ". Suggest to take her out to eat", specific,".")
    else:
        restaurant=input("Did your Girlfriend© tell you about some new restaurant she heard of ? (yes/no)").lower()
        if restaurant=="yes":
            specific=input("Which one exactly ?")
            print("Your Girlfriend© is hungry. Suggest to take her to", specific,".")
        else:
            print("It doesn't seem your Girlfriend© is starving. You can still ask her !")

Be careful when refusing to feed your Girlfriend©, as it may cause agressive behaviours.
If your girl is feeling sad, always go for Ben&Jerry's ice cream.
On her period, make reserves of chocolate-based sweets.
Your Girlfriend© might not want to bother and will sometimes not mention not having eaten, to prevent her for starving, ask her if she needs to eat, in case you had already eaten.

Emotional Needs

Your Girlfriend© is provided with cutting-edge emotional-system. Make sure to follow the instructions to fulfill her needs.

Your Girlfriend© is a highly complex structure and comes with a palette of emotions and feelings that vary upon their environment in intensity and frequency.
Here is a non-exhaustive list :

  • happy
  • sad
  • angry
  • excited
  • peaceful
  • stressed

Some of these emotions are negative. If you want to keep your Girlfriend© happy and peaceful, you should pay attention to her needs.
Your Girlfriend© needs to feel secure with you, prevent her from feeling lonely by making sure she feels loved.

Remember to text your Girlfriend© regularly, if you are far away, to let her know you too think about her.
If she ever tells you she feels sad, make sure to listen to your Girlfriend©'s feelings. Do not let your Girlfriend© alone in such difficult times.

Sometimes your Girlfriend© will be sad with no apparent reason. Do not blame yourself directly, it might be related to a lot of external factors.
Remember that you don't have to make your Girlfriend©'s problems disappear, however making her feel heard is already a way to show her she can count on you.

There are some moment where you have to be extra cautious with your Girlfriend© feelings.
These include:

  • Her period
  • Stressful professional or academic times
  • She didn't say a word for a while
  • You are not as accessible as you wished

Be careful around bed time, as your Girlfriend© might feel more emotional at night. If she tells you she doesn't feel right, it might not be ideal to fall alseep. Show her you care about her and encourage her to sleep while making sure she is ready to handle her emotions on her own.

In Case of Breakdown

Your Girlfriend© might be experiencing rough times and come to a breakdown. If this happens to your Girlfriend©, you should follow these guidelines

Your Girlfriend© has strong feelings that might lead her to burst out in tears unexpectedly. Be patient with your Girlfriend©, as this is a sign of emotional or physical distress that might have accumulated over time. To avoid this kind of situation, it is imperative to take care of your Girlfriend© correctly (ref. Emotional Needs).
Here are a few potential reasons for a breakdown :

  • You are emotionally unavailable to her
  • She didn't eat all day long (ref Feed Your Girlfriend)
  • She called her mom
  • You don't pay attention to her feelings
  • She is experiencing a general stressful time
  • You hurt her feelings without apologizing
  • You don't answer her texts / calls for a prolonged period of time

You also have your own needs and your Girlfriend© should not come across your boundaries. However, it may come to the point where both of you are simultaneously lacking regards for the other's needs. Keep in mind that it is not appropriate to always let your Girlfriend© prioritize your needs over hers. Each of you should be able to prioritize the other's needs from time to time.

Here's a programm to try to see if your Girlfriend© is having a breakdown:


cry=input("Has your Girlfriend© been crying for more than 5 minutes ?(yes/no) ").lower()
if cry=="yes":
    badcare=input("Did your Girlfriend© mention multiple time a lack of care, in the past few days ? (yes/no)").lower()
    if badcare=="yes":
        print("Your Girlfriend© is having a breakdown and needs your support.")
    else:
        print("Difficult to say. Your Girlfriend© might as well just be sweating a lot from her eyes")
else:
    print("You're all good !")

You should always make sure to take care of your Girlfriend© after a breakdown, as she will be in a weak state of mind afterwards. Never let your Girlfriend© deal with it on her own unless she told you so.
Here are some things you could do to help your Girlfriend© getting out of a breakdown.

  • Hug her
  • Make her eat and drink
  • Reassure her with words
  • Have sex
  • Listen to her
  • Show her you care

Never ever fall asleep during a breakdown as it gives the impression that your Girlfriend© is your least preoccupation. Do not use your phone during a breakdown as well. Your Girlfriend© is probably showing her feelings because she needs you to care.
