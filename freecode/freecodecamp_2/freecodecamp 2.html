David Bowie

Bowie throughout his eras, as Ziggy Stardust, during his Berliner Trilogy Era, and during the 90s.

Born to rock

Before Bowie came, David Jones, born on the 8th of January 1947, was already craving the spotlights. He studied in Bromley Technical Highschool, were he studied artistic and musical practices.

He founded his first rock 'n' roll band, the Konrads, in 1962 and has the ambition to become a pop star. He eventually left the Konrads for the King Bees with whom he'll only remain for less than a month before joining the Manish Boys.

David Jones made his first TV appearance on the BBC with the Manish boys in 1964. He presented himself as the founder of the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Long-Haired Men (a funny moment).

Odyssey through space

The first thing that might come in mind when mentionning Bowie is his fascination for space, which he displayed throughout his whole career.

From Space Oddity in 1969, to Blackstar in 2016, Bowie contributed largely to the space rock culture, which was a major musical movement in the 60-70s, with a specific focus on outfits and sounds.

He made himself famous with his alias personalities, such as Major Tom, an astronaut who'll eventually be stuck in his spaceship, Ziggy Stardust, an alien rockstar, Aladdin Sane...

Wonder in a modern world

Bowie was really well known for his novel look on technology and culture. His musical arrangements involved original instruments and techniques, such as with Space Oddity and its Mellotron. In his later career, he even used computer assisted techniques, for example for Hallo Spaceboy's lyrics writing .

His approach to art was ubiquituous and involved painting, filming, writing, music...

Bowie also understood the novelty of Internet, and created his owned Internet Provider named BowieNet. Even if the concept would eventually be extremely successful in the following, at the time it didn't really hit the spot, and the project was cancelled.

Into the rabbit hole

Bowie's career often related to mental health issues. His brother, as well as other members of his family were affected by schizophrenia. He often suffered from depression and always feared to also become mentally insane.

He expressed this fear by writing songs. Examples involve All The Madmen, The Prisoner, his persona of Aladdin Sane

However, he did put himself into states of controll loss, by consuming drugs throughout his whole career. This had an particularly big impact on his career as it created the persona of "The Thin White Duke", who was extremely controversial. His addiction to drugs led him to even more experimental music, with for example the album "Station to Station" which he didn't remember writing due to blackouts. But it also came with lifelong consequences and probably did have a role in his death from cancer.

Exiting the norms

David Bowie was known for his particular appearance in his early career, with his colorful looks as he portrayed his most famous character, Ziggy. Altogether, he did not care to pay respect to gender norms, and adopted an androgynous look. He is today an inspiration for artist like Harry Styles, who was on the cover of Vogue with a dress, just as Bowie posed in a dress for "The Man Who Sold The World"'s cover.

He also didn't mind to be open about his thoughts on queer culture, and did talk about his bisexuality multiple times.

Finally, he experimented a lot, with controversial lyrics in "China Girl", "I'm Afraid of America", "Ashes to Ashes". Bowie would always be there where he wasn't expected. He wrote his last album knowing about his upcoming death, and managed to surprise everybody with completely knew aesthetics, while remaining true to his past self, paying tribute to the start of his career, and fascination for space.
